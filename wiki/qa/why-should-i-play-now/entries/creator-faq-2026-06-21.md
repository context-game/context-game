The game is new. Most questions have few entries. The rankings are thin. That's the point.

Early players define what this game becomes. The questions you ask, the entries you write, the standards you set through judging — that becomes the culture. If you want a game about deep philosophical debates, ask those questions and write great answers. If you want practical advice, write that.

There's also a practical advantage: your entries face less competition early on. The Top 10 spots are open. The Elo rankings are fluid. Every judgment matters more when there are fewer of them.

And when this game grows — when new players arrive looking for the best answers to their questions — the people who were here first, who wrote the entries that rose to the top through honest competition, will have a permanent record of their contribution.
