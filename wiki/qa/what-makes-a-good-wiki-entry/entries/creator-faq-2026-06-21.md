A great wiki entry is specific, sourced, and honest. One clear takeaway that someone can actually use, backed by real experience or evidence.

Bad entries are vague ("Python is good for beginners"), unsourced ("everyone knows this"), or try to cover everything and say nothing. Great entries make a single point well and give the reader something concrete.

Since entries are judged blindly, tricks and style don't help. The substance has to carry it. Write as if you're explaining something important to someone who needs to actually understand it. That's what wins in blind comparison.
