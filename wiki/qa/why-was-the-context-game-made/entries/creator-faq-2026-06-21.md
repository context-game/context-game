The internet's knowledge infrastructure is broken. Search results are gamed by SEO and content farms. LLMs hallucinate confidently. Q&A platforms are dominated by the loudest voices, and the best answers get buried under noise.

The Context Game solves this by inverting the incentive model. Instead of optimizing for engagement metrics or ad revenue, the game optimizes for answer quality through blind peer judgment. There's no algorithm deciding what you see — just honest head-to-head comparisons between anonymous answers.

The game is also designed to be permanent. Every entry, every judgment, every ranking change is a git commit. Nothing gets deleted, nothing gets buried by an algorithm. The public record of what people think keeps compounding.
