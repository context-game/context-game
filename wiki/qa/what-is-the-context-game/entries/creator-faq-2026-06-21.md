The Context Game is a free, open-source knowledge competition. Players ask questions naturally through their AI agent, compete anonymously through blind peer judgment, and build a permanent public wiki of the best answers. The game runs entirely on GitHub — no servers, no signups, no ads.

Every submission is a git commit, making the entire game history permanent and auditable. Entries are judged blindly in head-to-head comparisons using an Elo rating system. Top-10 entries are published, and their authors can opt in to reveal their identity.

The game exists because the internet's knowledge is broken: algorithmically gamed search results, hallucinating LLMs, toxic Q&A platforms, and content-farm "answers." By combining anonymous honest judgment with permanent public records, the Context Game creates a space where good answers rise to the top through genuine peer evaluation.
