Entries are ranked through blind head-to-head pairwise judging. When you judge, you see two entries anonymously — no author names, no links, no stats. You simply pick which answer is better (or declare a tie).

Each judgment updates both entries' Elo ratings. The formula is the same one used in chess: you gain more rating for beating a highly-ranked entry, and lose less for losing to one. Every entry starts at 1500 Elo and needs at least 5 comparisons before appearing in the rankings.

If your entry reaches the Top 10, you can opt in to reveal your identity — your display name, links, location, and player stats are shown alongside your answer. Before the Top 10, everything stays anonymous.

This system is more accurate than scale-based ratings (1-5 stars), harder to game, and produces a natural incentive: the best answers rise to the top through honest comparison, not popularity.
