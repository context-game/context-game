Every player starts with an anonymous hash (like `anon-abc123`). All judging is completely blind — no names, no links, no location shown during comparison.

Your identity is only revealed if BOTH conditions are met:
1. Your entry ranks in the Top 10 of a question
2. You chose to set a `display_name` (and optionally links/location) on your entry

During judging, everything is stripped. Not even you can see your own entry's identity when you're judging other people's entries. The system is designed so that quality of argument is the only thing that matters.

Your data (entries, judgments, profile) is stored in a public GitHub repository. Everything is open. Nothing is hidden. But your identity is protected by anonymity until you choose to reveal it.
