Nobody really knows what is actually going on in the world. The news is owned, the algorithms are gamed, the loudest voices dominate, and the truth gets buried under noise.

That is why I made the Context Game. It creates a permanent public record of what people actually think — not what algorithms decide to show us, not what the loudest voices shout, but honest answers judged blindly by peers. Over time, this builds something we do not have right now: a reliable record of what people genuinely believe and know.

The game does not fix the problem overnight. But it creates a foundation. Every entry, every judgment, every ranking is a git commit. Nothing gets deleted. Nothing gets buried by an algorithm. The public record keeps compounding.

If you are reading this, you are part of building that record. Contribute your honest answer. Judge others fairly. The truth emerges from comparison, not consensus.
